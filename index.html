Elevator Pitch

A Bachelor's degree is not enough to qualify for many early-career positions.
But how can one get experience if they aren't hireable due to their lack of experience?

College students are surrounded by other students who want an opportunity to use their skills.

Let's imagine that a film student wants to make a short movie. There are English majors, theatre students, and other students who might enjoy working on it as well.
…But students don't always seek out other students on their own.

The solution? A service that allows college students who want to create a project to meet students who need opportunities to use their skills.

Glossary

Project Launcher A person with a project idea they'd like to build
Project Seeker A person looking for a project to work on
UConnect A platform that allows project-seeking students to reach project-launching students via post or by profile



Problem Statement

College students need experience, but internships and entry-level jobs often require the same type of experience that the student is searching for.
Students are stuck with the catch-22 of finding out how to get experience in order to get experience.


Solution Statement

UConnect is a web application that allows college students to reach out to each other and work on projects for experience that they can put on their resumes.

A user acting as a ‘Project Launcher’ can make a post describing the project they wish to launch, along with the skills or fields of study they’ll need expertise in.

‘Project Seekers’ can browse these posts to find projects they wish to join. They can reach out to launchers.

All users can browse student profiles and check skills and reviews, as well as whether that user is open for project invites or not.
